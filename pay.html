Learn how to protect yourself from fraud, phishing, and theft when making online transactions. Secure payments are essential for safe digital living.

Step 1: Use Secure Websites

Before entering sensitive details, check if the website starts with https:// and shows a padlock ðŸ”’ symbol in the address bar. Avoid suspicious links.

Step 2: Avoid Public Wi-Fi

Public Wi-Fi can be intercepted by hackers. Always use mobile data or a VPN when making online payments.

Step 3: Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) ensures that even if your password is stolen, your account remains protected via OTP or an authenticator app.

Step 4: Monitor Transactions

Regularly check your bank and credit card statements. Report any suspicious or unauthorized transactions immediately.

Step 5: Use Trusted Payment Gateways

Only use well-known and verified gateways like PayPal, Google Pay, PhonePe, or Razorpay. Avoid entering card details directly on unknown websites.
